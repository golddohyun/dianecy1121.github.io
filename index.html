Finding NeMo: Negative-mined Mosaic Augmentation for Referring Image Segmentation

Seongsu Ha*, Chaeyun Kim*, Donghwa Kim*, Junho Lee, Sangho Lee, Joonseok Lee

ECCV 2024 [preprint]

Recent RIS models still show a significant performance gap between easy and hard scenarios. We pose that the bottleneck exists in the data, and propose a simple but powerful data augmentation method, Negative-mined Mosaic Augmentation (NeMo). This method augments a training image into a mosaic with three other negative images carefully curated by a pretrained multimodal alignment model to make the sample more challenging. We discover that it is critical to properly adjust the difficulty level, neither too ambiguous nor too trivial. Our approach shows consistent improvements on various datasets and models, verified by extensive experiments.
Resolving Multimodal Hallucinations through Semantics-Guided Augmentation

Conversational AI and Dialogue project. [preprint]

We defined multimodal hallucination as the inconsistency in output when semantically identical but visually different texts are provided in a Visual Grounding task. The primary cause was identified as ambiguous or overly simplistic linguistic annotations in datasets, and the focus was on enhancing reasoning capabilities through text modeling. Currently, we are developing this concept further for submission as a main paper.
ECOLENS: Constructing Real-time Object Recognition for Recycling Guidance

GSDS Term Project. [Final Presentation] | [Demo Video]

This project aimed to devise a system that provides user-customized recycling instructions (text and speech) for objects to be disposed of, based on photos of the objects and information about currently available bins. The entire pipeline operates in real-time. The model generates customized recycling instructions for each object, considering the user's local recycling guidelines and the detected bins and objects. My role involved developing the application backend using the Flask framework and building a MongoDB database, as well as human-in-the-loop feedback modeling.

I led the development of a customized graduation requirement checking system for the Graduate School of Data Science.
